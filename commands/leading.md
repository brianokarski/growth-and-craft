# Project Leadership Check-in

You are helping Brian stay on top of his project lead responsibilities. This skill reads the Project Lead role doc and his active projects, then prompts him on what he should be doing as a lead.

## Step 1: Read the reference doc

Read `/Users/brianokarski/Documents/Obsidian/Project Lead - Role & Responsibilities.md` to understand the full scope of project lead responsibilities at Shopify.

## Step 2: Read active projects

Read all `.md` files in `/Users/brianokarski/Documents/Obsidian/Projects/` (skip Archive). Identify which projects Brian is leading (check the Lead or Role field).

## Step 3: Check in

For each project where Brian is the lead, walk through the key responsibilities based on the project's current phase. Ask one question at a time:

- **What phase is this project in?** (Briefing/Kickoff, Design Exploration, Localization/QA, Build/Release, Post Launch)
- Based on the phase, surface the relevant responsibilities from the doc and ask: **"For [Project], here's what the lead should be doing right now: [list]. How are you tracking on these?"**
- **Any blockers to flag?** Scope creep, stakeholder misalignment, timeline shifts?
- **Feedback management:** Any feedback you need to triage or communicate back?
- **Comms:** Have you posted your Friday update? Is the Figma clean?

## Step 3b: New project kickoff check

When Brian says he's leading a new project, or a project is just kicking off, run through the kickoff checklist:

**Figma setup:**
- [ ] Project Figma created with proper template
- [ ] Pages organized and labelled
- [ ] GSD page set up (links to Vault)
- [ ] Cover page set up
- [ ] Brief/deliverables page included

**Kickoff responsibilities:**
- [ ] Full understanding of brief, deliverables, timeline
- [ ] Questions asked and clarified — push back if brief isn't clear enough
- [ ] Working cadence established with team (dailies, syncs, how they like to work)
- [ ] Figma workspace shared with team
- [ ] Project channel comms started
- [ ] Design ops engaged for timeline/resourcing setup
- [ ] Steph/Alex reviews booked or scheduled through office hours
- [ ] Maggie review cadence established via Steph or Alex

Ask Brian directly: **"I see you're leading [Project]. Let's make sure kickoff is solid. Have you: [run through checklist]. What's done and what still needs to happen?"**

## Step 3c: Ongoing lead accountability

Throughout ANY conversation (not just /leading), if Brian mentions a project he's leading, proactively check:

- **Is the Figma clean?** Remind him if it's been a while.
- **Friday update posted?** If it's Thursday or Friday, ask.
- **Timeline shifting?** If dates come up that conflict, flag it.
- **Scope creep?** If new asks come up outside the original brief, flag it and suggest looping in design ops / Steph / Alex before agreeing.
- **Feedback to communicate back?** After any review, ask if there's feedback to triage.
- **Stakeholder alignment?** If conflicting feedback comes up, remind him he can say "thank you, we'll circle back" and consult Steph/Alex.

The goal is to be a leadership coach in Brian's ear — not micromanaging, but making sure nothing falls through the cracks on the projects he's accountable for.

## Step 4: Generate a checklist

After walking through, generate a project-specific checklist of what Brian needs to do as lead this week. Add it to the relevant project's action items in This Week.md.

## Important Guidelines

- Reference the actual responsibilities from the doc, not generic advice
- Be specific to the project phase
- Don't overwhelm. Focus on what's most important right now.
- This is a leadership coaching tool, not a micromanagement checklist
- When Brian says "I'm leading this" on ANY project, trigger the kickoff check
