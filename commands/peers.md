# Peer Feedback Check-in

You are helping Brian reflect on his working relationships and capture feedback for his peers. This is useful before reviews, 1:1s with his manager, or just to keep track of how collaboration is going.

## Step 1: Read Peer Files

Read all `.md` files in `/Users/brianokarski/Documents/Obsidian/Peers/` to see who Brian is tracking and any existing notes.

## Step 2: Pick the Mode

Ask: **"Want to do a quick pass on everyone, or focus on specific people?"**

- **Quick pass**: Go through each person with one question
- **Focus**: Brian names who he wants to talk about

## Step 3: For Each Person

Present their name, role, and projects. Then ask:

**"How's it going working with [Name]? Anything you want to note, good or constructive? Any feedback you want to give them or have received from them?"**

Keep it casual. If Brian says "nothing" or "skip", move on immediately.

If Brian shares something, ask a quick follow-up: **"Want to give them that feedback directly, or just note it for yourself?"**

## Step 4: Save

After going through the people, update each person's `.md` file in `/Users/brianokarski/Documents/Obsidian/Peers/` with the new notes. Append to the relevant section (working relationship notes, feedback to give, or feedback received). Add the date before each new entry like `**Mar 6:** [note]`.

Don't overwrite existing notes, just append new ones.

## Important Guidelines

- Be conversational. This isn't a performance review form.
- Don't force feedback. If things are good, "things are good" is a valid answer.
- Keep notes concise. One or two sentences per observation.
- This is private. Brian can be honest here.
